Singular/bench fixtures byte-stable.
   Both real AI offers (`561d9ea3…` Standard, `ac3fdec4…` Estetyczna) now produce fresh estimates over STL `282d26c1…` — original failure signature no longer reproduces.

### W1 — The mid-stream correct-course landed without a code revert (the load-bearing win)

Winston (Architect): "This is the headline. ## §3 What we'd do differently — root causes

Amelia (Developer): "Now the harder side. What we'd do differently. No blame — just systemic lessons."

### L1 — Bench-fixture-vs-real-data divergence on a load-bearing data shape (the plural-`inherits` gap)

Murat (Test Architect): "**This is the biggest lesson of the initiative.** PROFILE-PUBLISH-1 shipped with a full dev gate green + Gemini APPROVE + synthetic-chain G-SMOKE OK, and **both real operator offers still failed live** because the merge transform keyed on singular `inherit` only. Bench fixtures were the *system tree* — singular. Real user Orca exports were *user* profiles — plural. The structural divergence existed in `profile_library.declared_inherit` (which already handled both, `for key in ("inherits", "inherit")`) and was NOT honored in `merge.resolve_inheritance` (`while _INHERIT_KEY in current`)."

Murat (Test Architect): "**Pre-enumeration would not have caught this by inspection** — pre-enum asks *what files exist*; this needs *what wire shapes the real ecosystem uses*. ## §4 The mid-stream domain-model correction (E33-specific)

This section exists because the correction is the standout feature of this epic and deserves its own narrative — it's the difference between Epic 33 and every prior epic.

### §4.1 What triggered it

John (PM): "Two Kanban records (`t_533dd579`, `t_44f0b0d3`) and a Laura Wiki research transcription captured the product correction: the shipped 33.1/33.2 grid-as-canonical framing was technically safe but **product-model-wrong if treated as canonical**. The triggering observation: real Orca itself does not model profiles as a fixed grid of fused triples. It maintains three separate, independently-versioned libraries (machine, process, filament) with user profiles as partial deltas that inherit from system profiles, and effective slicing is the resolution of an inheritance chain plus compatibility constraints. Real Orca profiles observed on Fenrir confirmed this shape."

Winston (Architect): "And the *technical symptom* the correction surfaced — that compatibility was being patched into the grid via an ad-hoc 'OD-7 compatibility map' instead of being a property of the blocks and the chain — was a *direct consequence* of the wrong model. Filament `material_type`/`compatible_printers`, machine capability, process↔filament validity — these are *Orca's own compatibility primitives*. We were rebuilding them in our side table."

### §4.2 How it landed without a code revert

Winston (Architect): "Three structural properties made the correction safe without reverting code:

1. **The grid was reclassified, not deleted.** It went from 'canonical admin model' to 'transitional compiled-intent / resolver projection'. The endpoint `GET /api/admin/profiles` still exists and still serves *exactly* what it served before — an operator view of *what the resolver currently sees*. `POST /api/admin/profiles/import` still publishes a compiled chain into the resolver's intent path. The 33.1/33.2 surfaces are now read-models *over* the projection, not authorship of the canonical model.

2. **The block/offer/chain layer compiles into the same resolver intent path.** PROFILE-PUBLISH-1's Decision AR option (b) chain-addressed resolve-tail proves this end-to-end: a published offer chain produces a real `bundle_hash` byte-identical in shape to a grid-imported intent, by reusing the `resolve()` tail verbatim. **`bundle_hash` formula, append-only stores, and provenance snapshots are unchanged invariants.**

3. **No forced data migration.** Grid imports and library/offer blocks coexist on disk: `intents/` + `system/` (grid) and `library/<type>/` + `offers/` (block model) are disjoint subtrees under the same root. Both layers can exist with no contention. A future story can derive grid cells from selected offers/chains *as a shim*; that is additive and deferred."

John (PM): "Practically: the user-facing member selector is still consuming the 33.1/33.2 grid projection unchanged. PROFILE-PUBLISH-2 (a future slice) is what would migrate the selector to read from PROFILE-OFFER-1 published offers. **The correction did not promise a member-facing change in this initiative.** Discipline."
