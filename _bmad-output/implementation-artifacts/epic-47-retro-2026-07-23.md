## Decisions formalized at closeout

### Epic 47 is complete on its shipped scope — one fewer story than the original sketch, by explicit decision

The completion check on a literal story-count basis would see `47-4-category-api-surface-retirement: backlog` and flag the epic incomplete. That reading is wrong here: 47.4 carries an explicit `SUPERSEDED / ABSORBED → 47-5` marker with a dated, operator-approved correct-course record, the same relocation convention already established and accepted at E42's 42.3/42.5 closeout. 47.4 was never implementation-ready, never had tasks authored, and nothing shippable was ever built under that key — there is nothing outstanding to "finish." The epic's real deliverable count is four: 47.1, 47.2, 47.3, and the absorbed 47.5 (which carries 47.4's full original scope as internal phases (b)–(c) of its own atomic commit). `epic-47` → `done`; `epic-47-retrospective` → `done`; `47-4` stays `backlog`/historical, unchanged, not owed and not silently deleted.

### The correct-course judgment call: absorb, don't force

47.4's own spec-authoring correctly refused to guess when its stated precondition proved false — that is the "stop at unknown unknowns" discipline working as designed (`project-context.md` § AI agent execution discipline). The resulting decision — absorb 47.4 whole into 47.5 rather than force a two-deploy sequence with an intermediate live-outage window — was evaluated against two other options (direct standalone adjustment, rollback) and picked because it extended an already-approved principle (irreducibly-coupled category-removal work belongs in one atomic commit, per the 2026-07-19 E42 SCP) rather than contradicting it, and because it resolved the undocumented `ModelHero` breadcrumb gap the only way consistent with what Initiative 25 had already shipped: `TagGroupsSection` (Story 45.2) is the initiative's answer to "how do users see a model's classification," so plain removal with no replacement UI was the only option that didn't reopen a settled product decision.

### This is the third recurrence of the same drift class — the terminal cutover epic was not immune to it

The 2026-07-19 E42 sketch (42.3), the E43/E44 sketch-wording items, and now 47.4's "every consumer is gone after 44.3 + 45.x" precondition all shared the identical failure shape: an epic/story sketch asserting a dependency precondition that shipped code contradicted. The epic explicitly created to *fix* the class of destructive-cutover risk was not itself immune to the precondition-staleness drift the class shares — the third occurrence lands at the terminal cutover, the highest-stakes place it could have landed, and the same guardrail (the `bmad-create-story` spec-authoring self-block) caught it every single time, never a human noticing it first. This is folded into Action Items below with a concrete reinforcement, not silently closed.

## What went well

### 1. Category retirement's safety discipline held for five epics and one terminal cutover

The `Category` entity stayed a zero-code-cost compatibility bridge from E41 through E46 specifically so `main` remained deployable at every commit — and when the terminal destructive cutover finally came, it followed the full weight of gates the initiative had been building toward: explicit correct-course, an exact recorded destructive-go phrase, a verified-and-demonstrated (not merely existing) backup restore, full native + independent review, and a final terminal-UI operator approval. Nothing about the cutover was rushed or implicit. This is Initiative 25's central governance thesis proven out at the one point where getting it wrong would have been irreversible.

### 2. The correct-course response to a self-blocked story was the right call, not a workaround

47.4 blocking itself at spec-authoring — rather than an agent guessing past a false precondition — is the "stop at unknown unknowns" discipline catching a real problem before it became a live outage. The absorb-into-47.5 resolution didn't reopen the already-settled 45.3↔47.5 handshake or invent a new product decision for the `ModelHero` breadcrumb; it extended existing, already-approved reasoning (one atomic commit for irreducibly-coupled category removal) rather than contradicting it.

### 3. Review caught real operational remnants a code-only scan would have missed

Native CR-1 on `98246d7` found that `infra/scripts/cutover-smoke.sh` still probed the now-retired `/api/categories` endpoint (which would 404 and false-fail an unrelated auth-boundary smoke scenario) and that live `docs/operations.md`/`docs/architecture.md` still advertised the category surface as current. Both were repaired in `4636d88`: the smoke probe re-pointed to `/api/tags` (preserving the exact default-deny property the scenario verifies, with an inline comment explaining why), direct runtime 404/405 tests added for the retired routes, a stronger `AddModelForm` structural test, a bootstrap fixture given a unique slug, and the docs corrected to be honest about current state. An endpoint-retirement review that only checked application code would have missed all of this — it lives in operational tooling and prose documentation, not `apps/`.

### 4. Governance and test-authoring discipline held clean in this epic's final accepted evidence

No forged-reviewer-sign-off appears anywhere in 47.5's closeout record — every one of the 28 changed/added baseline contact sheets carries a genuine Laura/controller `ACCEPT`, and no agent pre-populated a human's approval before it happened. The pre-screenshot `toBeVisible()` discipline (named an action item after E45's 45.3 near-miss, partially missed once in E46's first admin screen) was checked at spec-authoring time in both 47.2 and 47.5's new visual specs, with no recurrence.

### 5. Recovery discipline preserved valuable work and avoided a second destructive action

Post-deploy closeout agent sessions hit `max_turns` during this epic. The controller inspected ATTENTION/done/deferred state and repo state directly rather than restarting from scratch, preserving already-valuable commits and resuming sessions when useful — and, critically, never re-ran the destructive `0019_drop_category` migration after the deploy had already succeeded. A `max_turns` cutoff after a successful commit and deploy is a truncated epilogue, not a failed unit; the docs-only recovery that followed did not endanger production at any point.

## Challenges / growth areas

### 1. ORCHESTRATION: `bmad-loop` exit code 0 is not the same as "done" — recurred repeatedly this epic

Consistent with the still-open E45 action item, `bmad-loop` runs during this epic's execution repeatedly exited 0 while the underlying story was still actually deferred or had hit `max_turns` mid-session. Two concrete new instances surfaced the same underlying environment-vs-status confusion in a fresh way:

- A missing worktree `node_modules` produced a **false-deferred** reading for 47.3 — the story looked stuck when the actual blocker was an unprovisioned dev-toolchain, not a product defect.
- A review worktree bootstrap helper that ignored its target path, combined with a worker venv initially built without `--extra dev`, produced a **false 13/16 gate** reading during 47.5's review cycle — the gate looked red when the actual cause was an incomplete environment, not a failing test.

Both were correctly diagnosed as environment problems (not product problems) and recovered without re-running any destructive step, which is the discipline working — but the underlying contract violation (an orchestration-layer signal that doesn't reliably distinguish "environment broken" from "story genuinely blocked" from "story actually done") is the same class of drift E45's action item already named, now with two fresh concrete instances at the highest-stakes epic in the initiative.

### 2. EVIDENCE PROVENANCE: a divergent uncommitted tree produced invalid "final" gate logs — correctly rejected, not silently accepted

An earlier, second-attempt round of what was presented as final closeout evidence turned out to have been generated against an uncommitted worktree that differed from the claimed HEAD by 73 files. Native review correctly rejected these logs rather than accepting stale or mismatched evidence, and the final logs actually cited in this retro (`check-all-e47-5-final2-4636d88-20260723_071245.log`, `determinism-e47-5-final-4636d88-20260723_072322.log`) were regenerated on the exact HEAD `4636d88` with an isolated venv before closeout proceeded. This is the review gate working correctly — but it means one full evidence-generation cycle was wasted on logs that could not be trusted, and nothing in the tooling currently prevents a future gate log from being generated against the wrong tree in the first place.

### 3. GOVERNANCE: this epic's clean result does not mean the systemic gap from E45/E46 is fixed

The E45-flagged forged-reviewer-sign-off defect (which recurred once already in E46, with a different human's name) did **not** recur in E47's final accepted evidence. That is a genuinely good outcome, but it is one clean epic sitting between two consecutive recurrences, not proof the mechanical gate-enforcement gap the action item asks for (reject a `baseline-reviewed: …, <name>` line where `<name>` ≠ commit author absent a provenance token) has actually been closed. The owner-side fix remains `open`, unchanged, outside 3d-portal. Reading this epic's clean result as "the gap is fixed" would be the same kind of premature-closure mistake this retrospective is explicitly instructed to avoid.

### 4. PROCESS: the terminal cutover epic recurred the exact drift class it exists to fix

Covered under Decisions above — 47.4's stale precondition is the third occurrence of a story/epic sketch asserting a dependency precondition that shipped code contradicted (after E42's 42.3 sketch and the E43/E44 sketch-wording items). The one guardrail that has caught all three occurrences is the `bmad-create-story` spec-authoring dependency audit — a procedural check exercised at story-authoring time, not a decomposition-time discipline that prevents the stale assertion from being written into a sketch in the first place. That decomposition-time gap is what Action Items below concretely reinforces.

## Key takeaways

1. **A five-epic-long compatibility bridge, held additive by discipline, is what made the terminal destructive cutover safe.** Category retirement's actual destructive step was one commit, at the very end, executed under the full weight of every governance gate the initiative had built toward it. That sequencing — additive first, destructive only once, at the end, under maximum ceremony — is the initiative's central lesson and it held all the way through.
2. **The guardrail that caught the terminal epic's own precondition drift is a story-authoring-time check, not a decomposition-time one.** Three recurrences of the same stale-precondition class (E42, E43/E44, E47) were all caught by `bmad-create-story`'s spec-authoring dependency audit, never by a human noticing first and never by the epic sketch itself. The fix belongs earlier in the pipeline — reinforced as a concrete action item, not silently closed.
3. **A clean epoch is not the same as a closed gap.** No forged reviewer sign-off appears in E47's final accepted evidence, after two consecutive recurrences in E45 and E46 — a genuinely good outcome, but explicitly not evidence the underlying mechanical enforcement gap is fixed. The action item stays open, unchanged.
4. **Evidence rejected for being generated against the wrong tree is the system working, but it is not free.** A 73-file-divergent uncommitted worktree produced "final" logs that native review correctly refused to accept. The fix is to make the failure mode detectable from the log itself (self-recorded HEAD + dirty-state hash) rather than relying on a reviewer to notice the mismatch after the fact.
5. **Environment failures and product failures can look identical from the outside — the correct response was to diagnose, not to assume.** A missing `node_modules` and a worker venv without `--extra dev` each produced a misleading readiness signal (false-deferred, false 13/16); both were correctly traced to environment causes and recovered without touching product code or re-running the destructive migration.
6. **Recovery discipline protected production twice: by not restarting valuable work, and by never re-running a destructive step that had already succeeded.** `max_turns` terminal conditions after successful commits were treated as truncated epilogues, not failed units — the controller inspected state and recovered rather than guessing.
